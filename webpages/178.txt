Burkina Faso’s Army Backs Zida as Leader
Government’s ‘Form and Duration Will Be Determined Later,’ Military Says

 






By

Drew Hinshaw


Drew Hinshaw
The Wall Street Journal

drew.hinshaw@wsj.com

Burkina Faso’s military declared a junior officer head of state Saturday, in a further indication that the poor, landlocked West African country—one of the region’s longest-running democracies—is now under military rule. A six-paragraph statement from the country’s armed forces named Lt. Col. Isaac Zida leader of a government “whose form and duration will be determined later.” It was signed by Gen.
