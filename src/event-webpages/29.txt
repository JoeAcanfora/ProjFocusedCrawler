      Philippines Typhoon: IRC responds to Hagupit    



December 6, 2014 by
    The IRC        











		                
                  Typhoon Hagupit, detail of satellite image from December 3, 2014.                                      Photo: NRL Marine Meterology Division, licensed under public domain via Wikimedia Commons                           



Published Dec. 3. Last updated Dec. 9.Typhoon Hagupit (known locally as Typhoon Ruby) made landfall in the Philippines on Saturday after at least one million people were evacuated from their homes. The International Rescue Committee prepared for the worst, positioning resources in the region for emergency relief and providing technical support to our local partners. We rolled out emergency teams across the country to assess damage and provide necessary relief.Many areas of the archipelago nation are still struggling to recover a year after one of the strongest storms in recorded history, Typhoon Haiyan, disrupted the lives of more than 14 million people. The IRC deployed its emergency response team immediately after the storm subsided.
