TALLAHASSEE - The sister of one of the victims shot at the Florida State University library last week told reporters her brother has been upgraded to serious condition, but is unable to walk.Farhan "Ronny" Ahmed was  injured after being shot three times in front of Strozier Libary. The first bullet injured his spinal cord, said Farhana Ahmed, Ronny's sister.   RELATED: SOCIAL MEDIA TELLS STORY OF FSU SHOOTINGS  Friends of the 21-year-old student, who is being treated at Tallahassee Memorial HealthCare, have set up a fundraising website, which has already raised over $30,000.   He was shot outside Strozier Library, where about 450 students were studying just before 12:30 Thursday morning, when Myron May, 31, showed up and opened fire. When police arrived, the alumnus had wounded two students and an employee and reloaded a .380 semi-automatic pistol.  	Elijah Valdez, 18, was grazed by a bullet, treated on scene and released. Nathan Scott, 30, was being treated at Tallahassee Memorial HealthCare, along with Ahmed.  	   	May refused to put down his weapon and a gun battle erupted. Between May and police, 30 rounds were fired.  	   	May graduated from Florida State in 2005 and earned a law degree at Texas Tech University. He worked as a lawyer in Texas and New Mexico before returning about three weeks ago to Florida. He has family in Ohio, where he was born.  	   	Police obtained videos and journals Thursday that indicate May feared he was being watched and targeted by the government Click the link for the fundraising page set up for Farhan Ahmed.
