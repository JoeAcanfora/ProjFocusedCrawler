Updated Dec. 5, 2012 6:29 a.m. ET


Philippine disaster authorities struggled to cope with the devastation wrought by a typhoon that left hundreds of people dead or missing and drove tens of thousands from their homes, while officials tallied the economic damage to the already poor southern regions, which were the hardest-hit. Rescue teams tried to reach residents in places Typhoon Bopha churned into a sea of mud, upended trees, toppled electrical poles and flattened buildings.
