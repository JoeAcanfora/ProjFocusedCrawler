Typhoon Hagupit smashes into the Philippines | Typhoon Hagupit 




















Saturday, December 6, 2014




                            Views:
                          














Typhoon Hagupit weakened to a category three storm, two levels below a super typhoon, but could still unleash huge destruction with torrential rain and potentially disastrous storm surges of up to 4.5 metres, the weather bureau  said.
More than half a million people in the Philippines have fled from a powerful typhoon in one of the world's biggest peacetime evacuations as the storm churns towards central provinces that still bear the scars of a super typhoon 13 months ago. Typhoon Hagupit Slams Into Philippines.
Typhoon Hagupit downed trees and knocked out power as it made landfall in the Philippines, but winds weakened slightly and there were no confirmed fatalities, authorities said.
Super Typhoon Hagupit began to lash the eastern Philippines with ferocious winds and heavy rain Saturday, knocking down trees and causing power outages as more than 600,000 coastal residents fled to safety.
Typhoon Hagupit has made landfall in the town of Dolores in the eastern Philippines, the country's weather agency says.
